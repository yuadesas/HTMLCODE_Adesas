Adventures in Code-Fu

My HTML Journey from Chaos to Cat Videos

Or, how I accidentally created a self-pacing cat video website (and almost lost my sanity)

Armed with boundless enthusiasm and zero coding experience, I embarked on my quest to conquer HTML
Little did I know my journey would be less "Lord of the Rings" and more :Three Stooges"

My first website lovingly named "Brenda's Blossomming Boutique" , resembled a digital Jackson Pollock painting.
Tags were scattered like confetti, colors clashed like a sunset on Mars, and the navigation system could only be
described as "whimsical" (by a very generous browser).

Determined to redeem myself, I tackled a "simple" project: a cat video gallery . What ensued was a hilarious
(and slightly terrifying) ballet of misplaced semicolons, rogue line breaks, and images that danced uncontrollably
across the screen. At one point, my entire website transformed into an auto-playing cat video slideshow,
much to the amusement (and slight concern) of my family.

After several meltdowns, countless tutorials, and gallons of coffee, I finally managed to wrangle my code into submission.
My cat video gallery wasn't exactly "elegant", but it functioned (mostly). And hey, at least I learned that even the clumsiest
coder can create something... interesting. So, here's to Brenda's Blossoming Boutique . the self- playing cat video extravaganza
and the never-ending quest to master the art of HTML.!
